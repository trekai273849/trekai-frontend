4-Day Detailed Itinerary

Follow the route through Patagonia with proper acclimatization and rest days

Day 1

Laguna de los Tres

📏 20km ⏱️ 8 hours 📈 +770m 🏔️ 1,170m

The classic Fitz Roy viewpoint hike. Start before dawn to catch sunrise illuminating the granite spires. Steep final kilometer leads to the glacial lake beneath Fitz Roy's east face.

Fitz Roy sunrise at Laguna de los TresGlacial lake with icebergs360-degree granite spire viewsRio Blanco base camp
Day 2

Laguna Torre

📏 18km ⏱️ 6 hours 📈 +300m 🏔️ 700m

Gentler hike to the base of the impossibly steep Cerro Torre. The trail follows the river valley through lenga forests to a stunning glacial lake surrounded by peaks.

Cerro Torre viewsGrande GlacierLaguna Torre with icebergsForest and valley hiking
Day 3

Pliegue Tumbado

📏 19km ⏱️ 7 hours 📈 +1090m 🏔️ 1,490m

Often overlooked but spectacular viewpoint offering the best panoramic views of the entire Fitz Roy and Torre ranges, plus the Southern Ice Field on clear days.

Panoramic views of entire rangeSouthern Patagonian Ice Field viewsCondor sightings commonLess crowded trail
Day 4

Loma del Pliegue Tumbado or Rest Day

📏 0km ⏱️ 0 hours 📈 0m 🏔️ 400m

Optional rest day or shorter hikes like Chorrillo del Salto (4km). Enjoy El Chaltén's cafes, microbreweries, and gear shops. Good weather backup day.

Essential Gear & Packing List

Proper gear is crucial for a safe and comfortable trek. Many items can be rented locally.

🎒 Essential Items

  • Hiking boots
    Sturdy with good traction
    Rental available
  • Wind/rain jacket
    Essential for Patagonian weather
    Rental available
  • Layering system
    Weather changes rapidly
  • Sun protection
    High UV exposure
  • Day pack
    20-30L capacity
    Rental available
  • Water bottles
    2L capacity minimum
  • Trekking poles
    Helpful for steep sections
    Rental available

👍 Recommended Items

  • Gaiters
    For muddy/snowy conditions
  • Headlamp
    For pre-dawn starts
  • Binoculars
    Condor and wildlife viewing
