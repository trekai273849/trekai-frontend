12-Day Detailed Itinerary

Follow the route through European Alps with proper acclimatization and rest days

Day 1

Chamonix to Argentière

📏 10km ⏱️ 4 hours 📈 +700m 🏔️ 1,252m

Gentle start along the Petit Balcon Sud with views across to the Mont Blanc massif. Good acclimatization day before the challenging days ahead.

Warm-up dayPetit Balcon Sud trailMont Blanc viewsArgentière glacier
Day 2

Argentière to Trient

📏 14km ⏱️ 6 hours 📈 +900m 🏔️ 1,279m

Cross into Switzerland via Col de Balme. Beautiful ridge walking with panoramic views before descending to the remote Trient valley.

Col de Balme crossingEnter SwitzerlandTrient Glacier viewsAlpine meadows
Day 3

Trient to Champex

📏 16km ⏱️ 6 hours 📈 +800m 🏔️ 1,466m

Choose between the spectacular but challenging Fenêtre d'Arpette or the easier Bovine route. Both lead to charming Champex-Lac.

Fenêtre d'Arpette optionBovine alternative routeChampex lakeSwiss villages
Day 4

Champex to Le Châble

📏 16km ⏱️ 5 hours 📈 +200m 🏔️ 821m

Easier day descending through forests and pastures to Le Châble. Option to take cable car to Verbier for supplies and comfort.

Val de Bagnes descentTraditional villagesVerbier optionRest day possibility
Day 5

Le Châble to Cabane du Mont Fort

📏 14km ⏱️ 7 hours 📈 +1636m 🏔️ 2,457m

Big climbing day to reach the spectacularly positioned Cabane du Mont Fort. The effort is rewarded with incredible sunset views.

Major ascent daySpectacular hut locationSunset viewsGrand Combin views
Day 6

Cabane du Mont Fort to Cabane de Prafleuri

📏 17km ⏱️ 7 hours 📈 +600m 🏔️ 2,662m

Remote and spectacular high mountain day crossing three cols. This is the heart of the Haute Route with incredible mountain scenery.

Remote high mountain dayThree cols crossingRosablanche viewsWild alpine terrain
Day 7

Cabane de Prafleuri to Arolla

📏 16km ⏱️ 6 hours 📈 +400m 🏔️ 1,998m

Descend from the high mountains to the traditional mountain village of Arolla. Good place for rest or resupply.

Descent to civilizationArolla valleyGlacier viewsTraditional mountain village
Day 8

Arolla to La Sage

📏 12km ⏱️ 5 hours 📈 +500m 🏔️ 1,667m

Cross the Pas de Chèvres with optional ladder section. Descend through traditional Valaisan villages to La Sage.

Pas de ChèvresTraditional villagesVal d'Hérens cultureMountain views
Day 9

La Sage to Cabane de Moiry

📏 14km ⏱️ 7 hours 📈 +1400m 🏔️ 2,825m

Another big day climbing to Col du Tsaté then traversing to the spectacular Cabane de Moiry beside the glacier.

Col du Tsaté crossingMoiry GlacierSpectacular hut locationHigh mountain environment
Day 10

Cabane de Moiry to Zinal

📏 14km ⏱️ 5 hours 📈 0m 🏔️ 1,675m

Descend alongside the Moiry Glacier to reach the beautiful Zinal valley, surrounded by 4000m peaks.

Glacier descentZinal valley4000m peaks viewsTraditional village
Day 11

Zinal to Gruben

📏 17km ⏱️ 9 hours 📈 +1300m 🏔️ 1,822m

One of the toughest days crossing the high Forcletta Pass into the German-speaking Turtmanntal. Remote and spectacular.

Forcletta Pass (2,874m)Enter German-speaking regionRemote TurtmanntalCultural transition
Day 12

Gruben to Zermatt

📏 18km ⏱️ 8 hours 📈 +1000m 🏔️ 1,608m

Final challenging day over Augstbord Pass. The first views of the Matterhorn signal journey's end as you descend to Zermatt.

Augstbord Pass (2,894m)First Matterhorn viewsArrival in ZermattJourney complete

Essential Gear & Packing List

Proper gear is crucial for a safe and comfortable trek. Many items can be rented locally.

🎒 Essential Items

  • Mountain boots
    Sturdy with good ankle support
  • Crampons
    Light crampons for snow patches
    Rental available
  • Ice axe
    For glacier sections (optional)
    Rental available
  • Backpack
    40-50L capacity
    Rental available
  • 4-season clothing
    Including warm layers
  • Waterproofs
    High quality essential
  • Navigation tools
    Map, compass, GPS
  • Emergency shelter
    Bivy or group shelter

👍 Recommended Items

  • Trekking poles
    Essential for stability
    Rental available
  • Gaiters
    For snow and scree
  • Glacier glasses
    Category 4 protection
