5-Day Detailed Itinerary

Follow the route through Patagonia with proper acclimatization and rest days

Day 1

Las Torres Base Trek

📏 22km ⏱️ 8 hours 📈 +800m 🏔️ 900m

The classic day hike to the base of the famous Torres. Start early for the steep climb through lenga forest, across mountain streams, and up the final boulder field to reach the stunning viewpoint of the three granite spires reflected in the turquoise lake.

Iconic three granite towersTurquoise glacial lake at baseAscenso Valley viewsBeech forest hiking
Day 2

Cuernos del Paine

📏 11km ⏱️ 5 hours 📈 +250m 🏔️ 350m

Scenic traverse along the turquoise waters of Nordenskjöld Lake with constantly changing views of the distinctive Cuernos (Horns) del Paine. The trail winds through diverse landscapes from grasslands to forests.

Views of Cuernos del Paine hornsNordenskjöld Lake shorelineVaried terrain and ecosystemsPotential condor sightings
Day 3

French Valley

📏 25km ⏱️ 10 hours 📈 +600m 🏔️ 550m

The longest day includes a detour into the spectacular French Valley. Hike up the valley surrounded by granite spires and hanging glaciers, with the option to reach Británico viewpoint for panoramic views, before continuing to Paine Grande.

French Valley hanging glaciers360-degree mountain amphitheaterAvalanche sounds from glaciersBritánico viewpoint option
Day 4

Grey Glacier Viewpoint

📏 11km ⏱️ 4 hours 📈 +200m 🏔️ 300m

Trek along Grey Lake's shoreline through burned and regenerating forest areas. Cross suspension bridges with views of floating icebergs and the massive Grey Glacier flowing from the Southern Patagonian Ice Field.

Grey Glacier viewsIcebergs in Grey LakeSuspension bridgesSouthern Ice Field connection
Day 5

Return via Catamaran

📏 0km ⏱️ 3 hours 📈 -280m 🏔️ 20m

Morning catamaran ride across Grey Lake among the icebergs, followed by bus transfer back to Puerto Natales. Alternatively, hike back to Paine Grande and take catamaran across Pehoé Lake.

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to the most common questions about this trek.

How far in advance should I book? +
For December-February, book refugios/campsites 5-6 months ahead. Shoulder seasons 2-3 months. The park has strict capacity limits.
Can I trek independently? +
Yes, the trails are well-marked and guides aren't required. However, weather can change rapidly, so experience with multi-day treks is recommended.
Camping or refugios? +
Refugios offer comfort and meals but are expensive. Camping is cheaper but means carrying more gear in challenging weather. Many do a mix.
What about the wind? +
Patagonian wind is legendary. Gusts can exceed 100km/h. Bring proper windproof gear and trekking poles for stability. Be prepared to wait out extreme conditions.
Is water safe to drink? +
Yes, water from streams and lakes is generally safe to drink without treatment, though some prefer to purify as a precaution.
