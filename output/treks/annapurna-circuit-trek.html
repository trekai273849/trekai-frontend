18-Day Detailed Itinerary

Follow the route through Nepal/Himalayas with proper acclimatization and rest days

Day 1

Drive from Kathmandu to Besisahar

📏 175km ⏱️ 6 hours 📈 -640m 🏔️ 760m

Early morning departure from Kathmandu for a scenic drive to Besisahar. The journey follows the Prithvi Highway before turning north along the Marsyangdi River valley.

Scenic drive through rural NepalViews of Ganesh HimalCross Trishuli RiverTraditional villages
Day 2

Trek to Bahundanda

📏 14km ⏱️ 6 hours 📈 +550m 🏔️ 1,310m

Begin trekking along the Marsyangdi River through subtropical forests and terraced fields. Pass through several Gurung and Brahmin villages.

First day of trekkingMarsyangdi River valleyTerraced fieldsMixed Hindu and Buddhist villages
Day 3

Trek to Chamje

📏 15km ⏱️ 6 hours 📈 +100m 🏔️ 1,410m

Trek through narrow gorge sections with the trail carved into cliff faces. Cross several suspension bridges and enjoy views of cascading waterfalls.

Dramatic gorge sectionsSuspension bridgesWaterfallsChanging vegetation
Day 4

Trek to Dharapani

📏 16km ⏱️ 7 hours 📈 +550m 🏔️ 1,960m

Steady climb through bamboo and rhododendron forests. Enter the Manang district at Koto, where Tibetan cultural influence becomes apparent.

Enter Manang districtViews of Annapurna IIPine forestsTibetan influenced architecture
Day 5

Trek to Chame

📏 15km ⏱️ 6 hours 📈 +750m 🏔️ 2,710m

Trek through dense forests with occasional clearings offering stunning views of Annapurna II. Chame is the administrative center of Manang district.

Annapurna II close-up viewsHot springs at KotoChame - district headquartersApple orchards
Day 6

Trek to Pisang

📏 14km ⏱️ 5 hours 📈 +590m 🏔️ 3,300m

Spectacular day walking through deep forests with the massive Paungda Danda rock face rising 1,500m above. Choose between Upper or Lower Pisang routes.

Paungda Danda rock faceFirst views of Annapurna IIIUpper and Lower PisangTraditional Buddhist monastery
Day 7

Trek to Manang

📏 19km ⏱️ 7 hours 📈 +219m 🏔️ 3,519m

Choose between the upper route through Ghyaru and Ngawal (highly recommended for views) or the lower valley route. Both converge at Manang.

Upper route via Ghyaru360-degree mountain panoramaTibetan Buddhist cultureManang village exploration
Day 8

Acclimatization Day in Manang

📏 0km ⏱️ 0 hours 📈 0m 🏔️ 3,519m

Essential acclimatization day. Options include hiking to Gangapurna Lake, visiting the monastery, or climbing to viewpoints above the village.

Hike to Gangapurna LakeVisit 500-year-old monasteryHRA health post lectureExplore Manang village
Day 9

Trek to Yak Kharka

📏 10km ⏱️ 4 hours 📈 +591m 🏔️ 4,110m

Leave the last major village behind as you enter high alpine terrain. The landscape becomes more barren with scattered juniper bushes.

Alpine terrain beginsYak pasturesStunning mountain viewsShorter day for acclimatization
Day 10

Trek to Thorong Phedi

📏 7km ⏱️ 4 hours 📈 +490m 🏔️ 4,600m

Short but steady climb to the base of Thorong La Pass. Rest early as tomorrow requires a very early start for the pass crossing.

Base of Thorong La PassDramatic mountain amphitheaterFinal preparations for pass crossingEarly rest for summit day
Day 11

Cross Thorong La Pass to Muktinath

📏 16km ⏱️ 9 hours 📈 +816m 🏔️ 3,800m

The big day! Start at 3-4 AM for the challenging climb to Thorong La Pass. Celebrate at the top before the long descent to Muktinath.

Thorong La Pass at 5,416mSunrise over the HimalayasAchievement of highest pointDescent to Muktinath temple
Day 12

Trek to Kagbeni

📏 16km ⏱️ 5 hours 📈 -990m 🏔️ 2,810m

Morning visit to the sacred Muktinath temple before descending through the dramatic landscape to the ancient village of Kagbeni.

Visit Muktinath templeEnter Mustang regionMedieval village of KagbeniKali Gandaki valley views
Day 13

Trek to Marpha

📏 16km ⏱️ 5 hours 📈 -140m 🏔️ 2,670m

Follow the Kali Gandaki River through the world's deepest gorge between Dhaulagiri and Annapurna. Marpha is famous for its apples.

Walk through world's deepest gorgeApple orchards of MarphaTraditional whitewashed villageApple products and brandy
Day 14

Trek to Kalopani

📏 17km ⏱️ 6 hours 📈 -140m 🏔️ 2,530m

Continue down the valley passing through traditional Thakali villages. The landscape transitions back to pine forests as you descend.

Pass through TukucheViews of DhaulagiriTraditional Thakali villagesPine forests return
Day 15

Trek to Tatopani

📏 18km ⏱️ 6 hours 📈 -1340m 🏔️ 1,190m

Long descent through changing vegetation zones. Tatopani means 'hot water' - enjoy the natural hot springs to soothe tired muscles.

Dramatic descentNatural hot springsSubtropical vegetationRelax sore muscles
Day 16

Trek to Ghorepani

📏 15km ⏱️ 7 hours 📈 +1670m 🏔️ 2,860m

Challenging climb up stone steps through terraced fields and rhododendron forests to reach Ghorepani, gateway to Poon Hill.

Steep climb through villagesRhododendron forestsMagar and Gurung culturePrepare for Poon Hill sunrise
Day 17

Poon Hill Sunrise, Trek to Nayapul, Drive to Pokhara

📏 20km ⏱️ 8 hours 📈 -2038m 🏔️ 822m

Pre-dawn hike to Poon Hill for spectacular sunrise over the Himalayas. Descend to Nayapul and drive to Pokhara to complete the circuit.

Sunrise from Poon Hill (3,210m)Panoramic mountain viewsFinal descent to roadArrive in Pokhara

Essential Gear & Packing List

Proper gear is crucial for a safe and comfortable trek. Many items can be rented locally.

🎒 Essential Items

  • Trekking boots
    Waterproof with good ankle support
    Rental available
  • Sleeping bag
    -20°C rated for Thorong La Pass
    Rental available
  • Down jacket
    Essential for pass crossing
    Rental available
  • Thermal layers
    Top and bottom, 2 sets minimum
  • Waterproof jacket and pants
    Good quality rain gear
    Rental available
  • Trekking poles
    Highly recommended for pass day
    Rental available
  • Warm gloves
    Insulated for high altitude
  • Sun protection
    Hat, sunglasses, sunscreen SPF 50+
  • First aid kit
    Including altitude medication
  • Water purification
    Tablets or SteriPen

👍 Recommended Items

  • Gaiters
    For potential snow on pass
    Rental available
  • Down pants
    For extreme cold on pass day
    Rental available
  • Buff/neck warmer
    Wind protection
  • Headlamp
    Essential for pass day start
  • Crampons
    If crossing in winter/spring
    Rental available
