4-Day Detailed Itinerary

Follow the route through Peru/Andes with proper acclimatization and rest days

Day 1

Km 82 to Wayllabamba

📏 12km ⏱️ 6 hours 📈 +400m 🏔️ 3,000m

Begin at Km 82 after early morning pickup from Cusco. Cross the Urubamba River and hike through the valley with views of Veronica mountain. Visit Patallacta ruins before reaching camp.

Patallacta ruinsUrubamba River viewsFirst glimpse of snow peaksTraditional villages
Day 2

Wayllabamba to Pacaymayo

📏 11km ⏱️ 7 hours 📈 +1200m 🏔️ 3,600m

The most challenging day, climbing to Dead Woman's Pass at 4,200m. Start early through humid cloud forest, emerging above treeline for the final steep ascent. Spectacular views from the pass.

Dead Woman's Pass (4,200m)Cloud forest ecosystemLlamas and alpacasAchievement of highest point
Day 3

Pacaymayo to Wiñay Wayna

📏 16km ⏱️ 9 hours 📈 +400m 🏔️ 2,650m

Longest but most rewarding day passing through several archaeological sites. Cross the second pass at 3,950m, explore ancient ruins, and descend through stunning cloud forest to camp.

Runkurakay ruinsSayacmarca ruinsPhuyupatamarca ruinsCloud forest descent
Day 4

Wiñay Wayna to Machu Picchu

📏 4km ⏱️ 2 hours 📈 -220m 🏔️ 2,430m

Pre-dawn start to reach the Sun Gate for sunrise over Machu Picchu. Descend to the citadel for a guided tour of this Wonder of the World. Afternoon train back to Cusco.

Sun Gate first viewMachu Picchu sunriseGuided site tourOptional Huayna Picchu

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to the most common questions about this trek.

How fit do I need to be? +
Moderate fitness required. You'll be hiking 6-9 hours daily at altitude. Train with cardio and hill walking for 3 months prior.
Why must I book so far ahead? +
Only 500 permits issued daily (including staff). Popular dates sell out 6 months ahead. No permits sold independently.
What about altitude sickness? +
Common on day 2. Spend 2-3 days acclimatizing in Cusco. Go slowly, stay hydrated. Guides monitor everyone closely.
How heavy is my daypack? +
Porters carry up to 7kg of your gear. You carry only water, snacks, camera, rain gear - about 5-6kg.
Is the trail crowded? +
Yes, especially May-September. But the experience is still magical. Your group often hikes separately from others.
