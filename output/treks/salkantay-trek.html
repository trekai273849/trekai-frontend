5-Day Detailed Itinerary

Follow the route through Peru/Andes with proper acclimatization and rest days

Day 1

Cusco to Soraypampa

📏 12km ⏱️ 5 hours 📈 +500m 🏔️ 3,850m

Drive from Cusco to Mollepata, then to Challacancha. Trek through valleys to Soraypampa with optional afternoon hike to stunning turquoise Humantay Lake.

Humantay Lake optionFirst views of SalkantayApurimac ValleyAcclimatization hike
Day 2

Soraypampa to Chaullay

📏 22km ⏱️ 10 hours 📈 +750m 🏔️ 2,900m

The big day! Early start for the challenging climb to Salkantay Pass at 4,600m. Incredible views of sacred Salkantay peak before long descent into warmer cloud forest.

Salkantay Pass (4,600m)Salkantay peak close viewsDramatic ecosystem changeDescent to cloud forest
Day 3

Chaullay to Santa Teresa

📏 15km ⏱️ 6 hours 📈 -1350m 🏔️ 1,550m

Beautiful day hiking through cloud forest with increasing vegetation and warmth. Pass coffee and fruit plantations. Optional visit to Cocalmayo hot springs in afternoon.

Cloud forest biodiversityCoffee plantationsWaterfallsHot springs option
Day 4

Santa Teresa to Aguas Calientes

📏 16km ⏱️ 6 hours 📈 +490m 🏔️ 2,040m

Optional zipline before hiking to Hidroelectrica. Walk along train tracks to Aguas Calientes or take optional train. First distant views of Machu Picchu from Llactapata.

Llactapata ruinsFirst Machu Picchu viewsUrubamba RiverTrain tracks walk
Day 5

Machu Picchu Visit

📏 0km ⏱️ 8 hours 📈 +390m 🏔️ 2,430m

Early morning bus or hike to Machu Picchu for sunrise. Guided tour of the ancient citadel. Optional climb Huayna Picchu or Machu Picchu Mountain. Afternoon train to Cusco.

Machu Picchu sunriseGuided tour of citadelOptional Huayna PicchuReturn to Cusco

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to the most common questions about this trek.

Is it as good as the Inca Trail? +
Different but equally spectacular. More challenging, fewer ruins, but stunning mountain scenery and no permit stress. Many consider it superior for landscapes.
Can I do it independently? +
Yes, unlike the Inca Trail. But guided tours provide logistics, meals, and camping equipment. Solo requires carrying everything.
How cold does it get? +
Very cold at Salkantay Pass - can be -10°C at night. But by day 3 you're in warm cloud forest. Pack for all climates.
Is altitude sickness common? +
Yes, especially on pass day. Crucial to acclimatize in Cusco first. The rapid descent helps recovery.
